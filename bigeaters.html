BigEaters Food Case Study

Fundraising hub for UCI organizations and students

Sorry, this page is still under construction.

Project Definition

Bigeaters Food aims to provide students and guests an information on available food on the UCI campus, sold by club organizations. With its GPS-based map and easy-to-use interface, Bigeaters Food helps the users find the food options easily.

Business Problem

Selling food on campus is one of the best methods for organizations to fundraise money for their club. Conveiently, it is a cheaper option for students and guests to buy food between their daily activities. However, there has not been a tool that centralizes all the food available on campus daily. As a result, we proposed to create an informative application that allows club organizations to publicize their fundraisers, allowing students and guests to have access to the information of the food available on campus for the day.

Team Challenges

  • Research of the users were limited due to the structure of the course
  • Very little prior knowledge of developing mobile application
  • Team assemble was randomized
  • Members have a busy schedule with other responsibilities

Initial Approach

Gantt Chart

As a designated project manager for this project, I requested everyone to complete a Gantt chart of their personal schedule and collaboratively, worked on the Gantt chart of the project development process.

Personas

Mapping the journey (Process Flow)

I mapped out the individuals and structured my initial designs around the points of intersection between personas. This allowed the entire team to fully understand the scope of work being asked for and how the personas would depend on each other when interact with the application.

Defining a solution

Creating two interfaces

Based on the personas, the users are divided into two types: the club organization and the customer. Therefore, two interfaces are needed for these users. The club organizations, can use the application to publicize their fundraisers and promote their organization, while the customers, also-know-as the students and guests, can view the menu and find where the food are located.

Wireframes & Prototypes

We used Figma as a tool to collaborate online while we talk face-to-face to implement the features of the application. After the completion of the wireframes, we used Sketch as a tool to design different screens of our application, using pleasing colors, fonts, and feature placements.

Retrospective into our process

Not all is well that ends well

Due to our inexperience in mobile application development and a short working period, we were unable to make our prototypes into a functional mobile software. However, our knowledge and exploration of Sketch has helped paint a concrete picture of our vision to our Professor and Teaching Assistant. We were able to impress them with our innovative idea and presentation that we all received a great score for our project.

What we could have done differently

We definitely spent too much time on the documentations of the project because the course asked for them as assignments. As a design team, we must always be aware of the limitations and challenges that we have in regard to programming when implementing our vision. Further understanding of the users through user research and testing would have benefitted the effectiveness of our application.
